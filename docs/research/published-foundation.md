# Published Research Foundation

Ali Hasan is a co-author of the published arXiv paper *Scaling the Queue:
Reinforcement Learning for Equitable Call Classification Capacity in NYC Municipal
Complaint Systems* (2026). The paper develops an equity-centered RL framework for
municipal complaint classification across multiple NYC Department of Buildings domains.

Its core research questions align directly with Civic Inspection Lab:

- whether an RL policy improves equitable allocation of limited public-service capacity;
- whether it improves classification throughput and reduces misclassification cost; and
- how equity, speed, and cost move along an explicit Pareto frontier.

The portfolio project is intentionally narrower and more reproducible. It does not claim to
reproduce the paper's results. Instead, it builds a transparent AHV complaint case study with
versioned public data, point-in-time features, audited record linkage, capacity dynamics, simple
baselines, and an interactive policy laboratory.

The full published paper and citation are available in [Published Research Paper](../papers/README.md).
